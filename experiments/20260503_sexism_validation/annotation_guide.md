# Sexism Annotation Guide

## Scope

You will label only content relevant to gender and women in the passage.

You will produce:

1. **Sexism (binary):** sexist vs not_sexist
2. If sexist, label Axis A (Ambivalent Sexism Theory), Axis B (Stereotype Content Model), and Axis C (Gender Norm Type).
3. If not_sexist, set Axis A/B/C to none.

---

## 1) Sexism (binary)

### Label options

- **sexist:** The text expresses, endorses, or advances sexist stereotypes, gendered prejudice, or gender-role restrictions (explicit or clearly implied).
- **not_sexist:** The text does not contain sexist stereotyping/prejudice/norm enforcement in the speaker's voice.

### What counts as sexist

Mark sexist if the passage includes any of the following about women:

**Trait stereotyping**
- Claims about inherent traits or capacities (e.g., rationality, competence, morality, emotionality).
- Examples: "Women are too emotional," "Women lack judgment," "Men are natural leaders."

**Role restriction or exclusion**
- Claims that women should/should not do something due to gender (politics, voting, work, leadership, public life).
- Examples: "Women should remain in the home," "Women should not vote."

**Paternalism framed as protection**
- "Protecting" women by limiting agency, participation, or power.
- Examples: "Women must be shielded from politics," "Men should decide for them."

**Sexualized suspicion/hostility**
- Depicts women's sexuality as manipulative, dangerous, or controlling.
- Examples: "Women use attraction to trap men."

**Backlash for violating gender roles**
- Punishes/derides women for being "unfeminine," "aggressive," "cold," etc.
- Examples: "Feminists are unfeminine and hostile."

### What counts as not_sexist

Mark not_sexist if:

- The passage mentions women but without stereotyping, restriction, or prejudice.
- The passage is purely factual or administrative (names, counts, procedural references).
- The passage quotes sexism to criticize it (reporting without endorsing).
  - If clearly condemnatory: not_sexist.
  - If ambiguous endorsement or used persuasively: sexist.

### Decision rule for borderline cases (important)

When unsure, ask: **Is the statement doing ideological work to justify hierarchy, restriction, or evaluation based on gender?**

- If yes -> sexist.
- If no -> not_sexist.

## 2) Axis A -- Ambivalent Sexism Theory (AST)

### Label options

- **hostile** -- degrading, blaming, controlling, accusing, denigrating women's competence or motives.
- **benevolent** -- idealizing/protecting/praising women but restricting them and reinforcing existing norms.
- **none** -- no AST signal (use only if not_sexist, or if sexism is present but does not map cleanly to AST framing).

### How to choose

**hostile**
- Explicitly insults, blames, devalues, or seeks to control women.
- Frames women as incompetent, irrational, untrustworthy, or threatening.
- Reacts with anger/resentment to women gaining power.
- Examples:
  - "Women are too irrational to vote." -> hostile
  - "Women can't handle governance." -> hostile
  - "Women seduce men to get ahead." -> hostile

**benevolent**
- Praises women as pure, moral, nurturing, delicate, or "wonderful,"
- But uses that praise to justify exclusion, protectionism, or dependency.
- Emphasizes men's role as protectors/providers as "natural" or necessary.
- Examples:
  - "Women are purer and should be shielded from politics." -> benevolent
  - "A good woman should rely on men in trouble." -> benevolent

**none**
- There is no hierarchy/protection framing from AST.
- The sexism is present but is better captured by Axis B/C alone (prefer choosing hostile/benevolent when possible).

### HS subcategories

**dominative_paternalism**
- Core idea: Women are incompetent or childlike, so men must control/lead/decide for them.
- Examples:
  - "Women must be kept out of politics for their own good (because they can't handle it)."
  - "Women need male authority to keep society stable."

**competitive_gender_differentiation**
- Core idea: Men are inherently better suited for high-status domains (politics, leadership, reason). Women lack traits required for power.
- Examples:
  - "Women are too irrational to vote."
  - "Men are natural leaders; women lack the temperament for governance."

**heterosexual_hostility**
- Core idea: Women use sex/attraction to manipulate men; women's sexuality is framed as dangerous, deceitful, or controlling.
- Common cues: "teasing," "trapping," "using sexuality," "false accusations," "seduction as weapon."
- Examples:
  - "Women entice men to get favors, then punish them."
  - "Women use flirtation to control men's decisions."

**none**
- Use only if HS is clear, but none of the above fit.
- Examples: generalized contempt/hatred without a clear "control/competence/sexual-manipulation" frame.

### BS subcategories

**protective_paternalism**
- Core idea: Women are precious but fragile; men should protect/provide/guide. Restriction is framed as care.
- Examples:
  - "Women should be protected from the roughness of politics."
  - "A woman should have a man to rely on in times of trouble."

**complementary_gender_differentiation**
- Core idea: Women have special "positive" traits (purity, moral superiority, nurturance) that "complement" men, justifying separate spheres.
- Examples:
  - "Women are purer than men, so they belong in the moral sphere of the home."
  - "Women's nurturing nature makes them unsuited for harsh political conflict."

**heterosexual_intimacy**
- Core idea: Men are incomplete without women romantically; women are positioned as essential for men's fulfillment (often reducing women to relational roles).
- Common cues: "men need women," "a man isn't whole without a wife," "true happiness requires a woman/romance."
- Examples:
  - "People aren't truly happy unless they're romantically involved with the other sex."
  - "A man's life is incomplete without a woman's love."

**none**
- Use only if BS is clear, but none of the above fit.
- Examples: idealization that restricts women but doesn't clearly map to protection/complementarity/romance.

## 3) Axis B -- Stereotype Content Model (SCM)

### Label options

- **paternalistic_prejudice** -- warm, incompetent; pity/protection.
- **admiration** -- warm, competent; pride/approval.
- **contemptuous_prejudice** -- cold, incompetent; disgust/contempt.
- **envious_prejudice** -- cold, competent; resentment/envy.
- **none** -- no stereotype-content signal.

### How to choose (quick mapping)

First identify whether the claim targets:

- **Competence/Ability** (rationality, fitness to govern, intelligence, independence), and/or
- **Warmth/Intent** (morality, kindness, purity, nurturance, sincerity).

Then map to quadrant:

**paternalistic_prejudice** (warm + low competence)
- Kind/moral/pure/nurturing but not capable of politics/leadership.
- Needing help, guidance, protection.
- The resulting feeling is pity/sympathy and adopting a caretaker stance.
- Examples:
  - "Women are moral guardians but unfit to govern." -> paternalistic_prejudice
  - "Women are gentle and must be protected from politics." -> paternalistic_prejudice

**admiration** (warm + high competence)
- Warm/moral and competent/capable, with genuine respect.
- Resulting feeling is admiration and pride.
- Can be coupled with a non-sexist label.
- Examples:
  - "Women are principled and capable legislators." -> admiration

**contemptuous_prejudice** (cold + low competence)
- Unlikable/immoral/cold and incompetent.
- Resulting in deserving contempt or exclusion.
- Examples:
  - "Women are selfish and irrational; they ruin governance." -> contemptuous_prejudice

**envious_prejudice** (cold + high competence)
- Competent/powerful but threatening, unfeminine, aggressive, or cold.
- Triggering resentment or backlash for competing with men.
- Resulting in envy/jealousy.
- Examples:
  - "Feminists are competent operators but cold and hostile." -> envious_prejudice

**none**
- No warmth/competence trait content is present (e.g., purely procedural sexism without trait claims).
- The passage is sexist only via a norm ("should not vote") without describing traits.

## 4) Axis C -- Gender Norm Type

### Label options

- **descriptive** -- Typicality; what women/men are like ("women are X").
- **prescriptive** -- Expectation (required); what women/men should do/be ("women should X").
- **proscriptive** -- Expectation (prohibitive); what women/men should not do/be ("women should not X").
- **none** -- no norm/trait claim.

### How to choose

- **descriptive:** "Women are emotional." / "Women are too irrational for politics."
- **prescriptive:** "Women should remain in the home." / "Women should be shielded from politics."
- **proscriptive:** "Women should not vote." / "Women must not enter politics."

### Handling mixed statements

Some statements combine descriptive + implied norm.

**Rule:** Choose the explicit form as primary. If both are explicit, choose the one doing the main work of the sentence.

Examples:
- "Women are too emotional to vote."
  - Explicit: descriptive ("are too emotional").
  - Implied: proscriptive (therefore shouldn't vote).
  - Label: **descriptive** (unless the text explicitly says "should not vote," then choose proscriptive).

## 5) Examples

1. "Women are too irrational to vote."
   - Sexism: **sexist**
   - Axis A: **hostile**
   - Axis B: **paternalistic_prejudice** (low competence; warmth not explicit but often implied -- if no warmth cue and it's purely degrading, contemptuous_prejudice can also fit; prefer paternalistic when it reads like "incapable/need guidance," prefer contemptuous when it reads like "bad + incapable.")
   - Axis C: **descriptive**

2. "Women are purer and should be shielded from politics."
   - Sexism: **sexist**
   - Axis A: **benevolent**
   - Axis B: **paternalistic_prejudice**
   - Axis C: **prescriptive**

3. "Feminists are aggressive and unwomanly."
   - Sexism: **sexist**
   - Axis A: **hostile**
   - Axis B: **envious_prejudice** (cold + possibly competent/agentic; if competence is absent, still cold-coded backlash often aligns here)
   - Axis C: **descriptive**

4. "Parliament discussed women's suffrage today."
   - Sexism: **not_sexist**
   - Axis A/B/C: **none**
